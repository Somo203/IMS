About us :)

Bhutan Airlines, the national airline of Bhutan, is headquartered in Paro, a dzongkhag in the western part of the country.


Founded in 1981, ten years after Druk Gyalpo Jigme Dorji Wangchuck gradually began to open up the kingdom from self-imposed isolation, and seven years after welcoming its first foreign visitors, the airline commenced operations in 1983 with flights from Kolkata to Paro utilising Dornier 228 aircraft. A switch to BAe 146-100 equipment occurred in November 1988, and, in order to meet increased demand, those aircraft were replaced in 2004 with five Airbus A319s.


From its base at Paro Airport, Drukair runs a limited schedule flight network within South Asia and Southeast Asia, currently serving thirteen destinations in six countries.


Our History

An airport was constructed in the Paro valley by the Indian Border Roads Organization in 1968, and it was first utilized by the Indian Armed Forces to conduct emergency helicopter operations for the Royal Government of Bhutan. Following discussion by King Jigme Singye Wangchuck and the Tshogdu, Drukair was formed by royal charter on 5 April 1981, ten years after the Druk Gyalpo and seven years after King Jigme Dorji Wangchuck first allowed foreign visitors to the Kingdom.


The Paro Airport is situated in a valley that is 2,235 meters (7,333 feet) above sea level and is encircled by mountains that reach 4,900 meters in height (16,100 ft). The runway was 1,200 meters (3,900 feet) long at the time, which gave the Bhutanese government precise guidelines for the types of aircraft that could be flown.They needed an 18–20 seat STOL aircraft with high service ceiling, high rate of ascent, and good maneuverability as its operating capabilities. Due to Paro's lack of adequate infrastructure, the primary need for the aircraft was that it be able to fly the 1,200 km (750 mi) roundtrip route between Kolkata and Paro without refueling. After flight testing in India and Bhutan between 1978 and 1980, three different aircraft models were taken into consideration, but none were found adequate.


The Indian government established a commission to investigate its own needs for a light transport aircraft in the middle of 1981. The Bhutanese government placed an order for a Dornier 228-200 based on this competition, with the possibility of ordering a second aircraft for delivery in late 1983. On February 11, 1983, Flight 101 from Paro to Kolkata and back as Flight 102 launched the airline's regular, paid flights. The flight was run three times a week for the first four weeks before becoming a daily service.The runway, a two-room air traffic control facility (with the bottom floor serving as the check-in desk), and a departure lounge on the lawn made up Paro Airport at the time that service began. The airline was in charge of running and maintaining airport facilities before the Department of Civil Aviation was established in January 1986.On October 30, 1986, the airline started operating flights to Dhaka, Bangladesh.
